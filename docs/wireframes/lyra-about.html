Built by people who've lived programme delivery

Lyra was born from years of first-hand experience managing some of the most complex transformation programmes in the UK. We know what works — and what doesn't.

Our story

Lyra started with a simple observation: the tools available to programme managers weren't designed for them. Spreadsheets, legacy planning tools, and generic project management apps all fall short when you're coordinating dozens of workstreams across multiple portfolios.

We spent years navigating these challenges first-hand — running transformation programmes, managing dependencies across teams, and spending hours formatting reports that should have taken minutes.

Lyra is the tool we wished existed. Purpose-built for the complexity of real programme delivery, with intelligence baked in at every level.

We spent more time fighting the tools than fighting the problems. Lyra changes that.
— Lyra founding team

What drives us

Five principles that shape every feature we build and every decision we make.

🎯

Flexibility First

Every organisation delivers differently. Lyra adapts to your structure, not the other way around. Configure hierarchy, columns, thresholds, and workflows to match how you work.

📊

Boardroom Ready

Executive reporting has to look world-class. Every export, every chart, every dashboard is designed to be shared with confidence at the highest levels.

🔒

Real Governance

Change control isn't a checkbox — it's a workflow. Lyra gives you genuine baselining, threshold-based alerts, approval chains, and a complete audit trail.

🔗

Useful Dependencies

Dependencies should illuminate the critical path, not just clutter the view. A dedicated workspace lets you model relationships properly before committing.

🤖

AI That Helps

AI generates first-draft plans from your documents, writes risk commentary, and aggregates status — saving hours of admin for PMs and workstream leads.

⚡

Speed Matters

A planning tool must be fast. Lyra is built for real-time responsiveness — no waiting for pages to load, no spinning wheels while your Gantt renders.
